## Full Description

**The Problem:**
Mobile game ads show satisfying puzzle gameplay. But when you download the game, it's something completely different. We've all been there.

**The Solution:**
Mobile Gaming delivers exactly what those ads promised - four browser-based puzzle games with honest gameplay:

💧 **Water Sort** - Pour colored liquids between tubes to sort them. Simple concept, increasingly complex puzzles.

🚗 **Parking Escape** - The classic unblock puzzle. Slide cars to clear a path for the exit. Rush Hour vibes.

🚌 **Bus Jam** - Passengers wait in color-coded lines. Load matching passengers onto buses. Strategic color matching.

📌 **Pull the Pin** - Remove pins in the correct order to guide balls to their destination. Physics-based puzzle solving.

**Why You'll Love It:**

- **No Bait-and-Switch** - What you see is what you get
- **No Ads** - Zero interruptions
- **No Tracking** - Your data stays yours
- **No Download** - Play instantly in browser
- **Works Offline** - Install as PWA for offline play
- **Cross-Platform** - Works on desktop, mobile, tablet
- **100+ Levels** - Per game, with more coming
- **Infinite Mode** - Procedurally generated puzzles

**Built for Puzzle Lovers:**
If you've ever watched a mobile game ad and thought "I want to play THAT game," this is for you.

Free forever. No catch.

Play now: mobile-gaming.pages.dev

## Maker Comment

Hey Product Hunt! 👋

I built Mobile Gaming after one too many experiences with mobile game ads that showed satisfying puzzles but delivered completely different games.

The breaking point was a water-sorting puzzle ad. I downloaded the game immediately... only to find it was yet another match-3 clone with energy systems and ads every 30 seconds.

So I built what the ad promised.

Mobile Gaming is four browser-based puzzle games that deliver exactly what you see:
- Water Sort (liquid pouring puzzles)
- Parking Escape (car sliding puzzles)
- Bus Jam (color-matching puzzles)
- Pull the Pin (physics puzzles)

No ads, no tracking, no paywalls, no energy systems. Just puzzles.

I'd love to hear your feedback! What puzzle types should I add next?

Happy to answer any questions about the development or design decisions.
